# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. It has increased rapidly with the growth of digital payments in India.

A typical UPI fraud happens step-by-step. First, the attacker contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency or offer an attractive deal, such as a cashback or prize. Then, they send a “collect request” or a malicious link and instruct the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is needed to receive money. However, entering the PIN actually approves a payment from the victim’s account. In some cases, scammers also ask users to install screen-sharing apps to steal sensitive information.

The primary targets are students, elderly people, and individuals who are new to digital payments, as they may lack awareness of how UPI works.

The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose money instantly, and recovery is often difficult. Additionally, such incidents cause stress, financial insecurity, and loss of trust in online systems. In some cases, personal data may also be misused for further fraud.

This case highlights the importance of awareness. Users should never share their UPI PIN, approve unknown requests, or trust unsolicited calls related to financial transactions.
